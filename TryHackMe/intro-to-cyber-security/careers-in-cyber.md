## Why I did this room

Before I start throwing myself into technical labs, I wanted to understand the landscape. what roles actually exist, what people do day to day, and where I fit in. This room answered all of that.

---

## What the room covers

Four career paths, explained one by one:

### Task 1 : Introduction

The room opens with something I needed to hear: **you don't need a specific background to start in cybersecurity.** If you enjoy problem solving, curiosity, and figuring out how things work there's a role for you.

Three reasons the field is worth getting into:
- Over **3.5 million unfilled roles** globally right now
- **Strong salaries** even at entry level
- **Constant learning** the field never gets boring because it never stops changing

> **Q: How many unfilled cyber positions are there?**  
> **A: 3.5 million**

---

### Task 2 : Security Analysis (the role I want)

This is the one that spoke to me. Security Analysts are described as the **"digital defenders"** of an organisation. They sit on the blue team and their job is to monitor, investigate, and respond.

What a typical day looks like:
- Monitoring activity on devices and across the network
- Investigating unusual behaviour, like someone in London suddenly logging in from another country
- Piecing together information to understand what happened, when it happened, and how
- Working with other teams to make the organisation's defences stronger

The progression from Security Analyst goes toward Threat Hunting, Incident Response, and Malware Analysis — all things I want to explore eventually.

> **Q: Security analysts play a significant role in an organisation's ____?**  
> **A: defence**

---

### Task 3 : Security Engineering

Security Engineers are the architects, they build and maintain the systems that protect an organisation. Think of them as the people who design the security infrastructure that analysts like me will eventually work within.

One thing I didn't know before this task: Security Engineers are responsible for maintaining **Intrusion Detection Systems (IDS)** — basically digital security cameras that use rules and intelligence to detect attacks. Knowing this helped me understand where the alerts I'll be triaging actually come from.

> **Q: What device would a Security Engineer be responsible for maintaining?**  
> **A: Intrusion Detection System**

---

### Task 4 : Penetration Testing

Pentesters are the ethical hackers. They're hired to legally try to break into a company's systems. Everything done under a formal written agreement called an **engagement**.

What I found interesting: penetration testing is a career path that eventually leads into **Red Teaming**, where you simulate full-scale attacks against an entire organisation, sometimes even including physical intrusion. That's a completely different world from SOC work, but understanding what pentesters do actually makes you a better defender, because you know how the attacks work.

> **Q: What process do pentesters follow when testing an organisation?**  
> **A: engagement**

---

## What I took away from this room

A few things that stuck with me:

1. **SOC Analyst is a broad entry point** you start by monitoring and triaging, then specialise into whatever interests you most. Threat hunting, incident response, malware analysis, all realistic progressions.

2. **The roles are interconnected** ,the Security Engineer builds the IDS that generates the alerts I'll investigate. The Pentester uses the same techniques that attackers use and that I'll be detecting. Everyone's work feeds into everyone else's.

3. **3.5 million unfilled roles is not a small number** ,the demand is genuinely there for people willing to put in the work to build real skills.

---

## How this connects to SOC work

Everything in this room pointed me toward the same conclusion: the SOC Analyst role is the right starting point for me. It's where you build the broadest foundation, networking, threat detection, SIEM, log analysis, incident response, before specialising. And the demand for people who can do it well is massive.

I'm building toward this role deliberately. TryHackMe → Security+ → SOC Level 1 path → first job. This room helped me confirm that plan makes sense.
